1. What is Good Sleep? 😴

Good sleep means:

  • ⏳ Fall asleep within ~20 minutes
  • 💤 Sleep deeply with few wake-ups
  • 🌅 Wake up refreshed, not groggy

👉 Most adults need at least 7–9 hours, but aiming for 8 hours or more helps you feel your best.
👉 Teens need 8–10 hours to support growth and learning.
📖 CDC (2022); American Academy of Sleep Medicine

2. Why is Good Sleep Important?

  • 🧠 Boosts learning & memory (Diekelmann & Born, 2010)
  • 🎯 Improves focus & problem-solving (Lo et al., 2016)
  • 🛡️ Supports immune health (Besedovsky et al., 2019)
  • 😊 Balances hormones & mood (Medic, Wille, & Hemels, 2017)

3. What Happens If We Don’t Get Enough Sleep?

  • 😴 Daytime fatigue & poor focus (Durmer & Dinges, 2005)
  • 🧠 Weakened memory & learning (Diekelmann & Born, 2010)
  • 😡 Mood swings & stress (Walker, 2017)
  • 🛡️ Weaker immunity (Besedovsky et al., 2019)
  • ❤️ Long-term health risks (Medic, Wille, & Hemels, 2017)

4. What Happens If We Don’t Follow the Right Sleep Cycle?

  • 🌀 Grogginess if waking mid-cycle (Tassi & Muzet, 2000)
  • ⚖️ Hormone disruption (Van Cauter et al., 2000)
  • ✨ Poor memory & creativity (Rasch & Born, 2013)
  • 📉 Sleep debt & irregular cycles (Banks & Dinges, 2007)
